Kenneth Daily is a Senior Scientist in the data science group at Sage Bionetworks in Seattle, WA.

My interests are in open data and research in the life sciences. I help consortiums collaborate and share data, contribute to our platform Synapse, and teach about bioinformatics and open research.

Previously, I completed a postdoctoral fellowship in the Dermatology Branch of the Center for Cancer Research of the National Cancer Institute in the Lab of Dr. Isaac Brownell. I focused on the genomic analysis of Merkel cell carcinoma, a rare, viral associated, aggressive neuroendocrine skin cancer. I integrated a number of high-throughput molecular analyses on tissue samples and cell lines from MCC tumors to learn about the mechanisms of tranformation, identify recurrent somatic mutations, identify druggable targets, and develop a pre-clinical mouse model.

I earned my Ph.D. in Computer Science at the University of California, Irvine and my B.S and M.S. degrees in Informatics from Indiana University.
